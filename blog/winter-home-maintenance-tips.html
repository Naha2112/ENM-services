5 Essential Winter Home Maintenance Tips

December 15, 2024 Property Maintenance 5 min read

Protect your home this winter with these essential maintenance tasks from London's trusted property maintenance experts.

As winter approaches in London and the surrounding areas, it's crucial to prepare your home for the colder months ahead. At ENM'S SERVICES, we've helped countless homeowners protect their properties from winter damage. Here are our top 5 essential maintenance tips to keep your home safe, warm, and efficient throughout the winter season.

1. Service Your Heating System

Your heating system will be working overtime during winter, so it's essential to ensure it's running efficiently and safely.

  • Schedule a professional boiler service: Have a qualified engineer inspect your boiler, clean components, and check for any potential issues.
  • Replace filters: Clean or replace heating system filters to improve efficiency and air quality.
  • Bleed radiators: Remove trapped air from radiators to ensure even heat distribution throughout your home.
  • Check thermostats: Ensure your thermostat is working correctly and consider upgrading to a programmable model for better energy efficiency.

2. Insulate and Protect Your Pipes

Frozen pipes can cause thousands of pounds in damage. Prevention is always better than cure when it comes to pipe protection.

  • Insulate exposed pipes: Wrap pipes in unheated areas like basements, attics, and garages with pipe insulation.
  • Seal gaps and cracks: Use caulk or expanding foam to seal gaps around pipes where they enter your home.
  • Know your stopcock location: Ensure everyone in your household knows where the main water shut-off valve is located.
  • Keep heating on: Even when away, maintain a minimum temperature of 12Â°C to prevent freezing.

3. Clean and Inspect Your Gutters

Clogged gutters can lead to ice dams, water damage, and structural issues during winter months.

  • Remove debris: Clear leaves, twigs, and other debris from gutters and downspouts.
  • Check for damage: Look for cracks, loose brackets, or sagging sections that need repair.
  • Ensure proper drainage: Make sure water flows freely through downspouts and away from your foundation.
  • Consider gutter guards: Install guards to reduce future maintenance and prevent clogging.

4. Seal Windows and Doors

Proper sealing can significantly reduce heating costs and improve comfort during winter.

  • Check weatherstripping: Inspect and replace worn weatherstripping around doors and windows.
  • Caulk gaps: Apply fresh caulk around window frames and door frames to prevent drafts.
  • Install storm windows: Consider adding storm windows for extra insulation in older homes.
  • Use draft excluders: Place draft excluders at the bottom of doors to prevent cold air infiltration.

5. Prepare Your Roof and Exterior

Your roof and exterior walls are your home's first line of defense against winter weather.

  • Inspect roof tiles: Look for loose, damaged, or missing tiles that could allow water infiltration.
  • Check flashing: Ensure flashing around chimneys, vents, and skylights is secure and watertight.
  • Trim tree branches: Remove branches that could fall on your roof during winter storms.
  • Inspect exterior walls: Look for cracks or gaps in exterior walls and seal them to prevent moisture and pest infiltration.

Professional Tip

Don't wait until the first freeze to address these maintenance tasks. Start your winter preparations in early autumn when weather conditions are still favorable for outdoor work.
